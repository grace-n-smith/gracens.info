DOILYBOWL - An exercise in flexible prints and heading towards mass-producibility

Design was inspired by a need for packaging for chocolates. The doilybowls print fast and flat in two layers and then are thermoformed with the help of a hairdryer/heater and two mold pieces.

Cable chain and mount mod for Kobra Max

The Kobra Max large-format 3D printer comes with cery poor cable management, to the point where the printer will, over time, fatigue it's own cables near the connectors. This modular cable chain keeps them neat and out of the way, and protects against snags and pulls.

Light-Duty Curtain Rods

For a while I discouraged myself from printing long thin projects because it seemed best to print them vertically, and that resulted in parts that were prone to snapping in use (or even breaking during assembly). I got a Kobra Max 3d printer, and with its 400x400mm printbed, my dreams suddenly got bigger. This 110cm curtain rod is printed in two segments and assembled with a small collar in the center. It lightly snaps into the wall mounts to ensure that there's no movement when I draw the curtains. Printed without supports.



Selen Tatsuki Costume Element

This is the charm that hangs off of Selen Tatsuki's belt in her V2 model. The design inspired me to create a 3d-printable model that can be used for cosplay. I recommend printing it in two identical halves that fit together, and then electroplating the print in silver.

Brass Ultrasonic Probe

A sonication probe that I made out of brass on a beautiful vintage manual lathe. It was used to take a liquid-metal catalyst and break it into tiny spheres in a solvent, making a "liquid-metal-and-solvent vinaigrette". This drastically increases the surface area of the catalyst for the formation of graphene sheets on the surface of the metal. The probe was made slightly longer than the vibration node calculation suggested, and then sanded down until it hit resonance. I worked full-time on this carbon sequestration project in the summer of 2022.
